ii.
02 / 06The Six Phases

From discovery to handover.

Six phases, each ending in a hard approval gate. No phase begins until its predecessor's gate is signed, paid, and logged in the Decisions Database. This is the spine that holds 4 to 6 months together.

A

Discovery & Onboarding

Wk 0 — 2

Qualify, listen, measure, contract. The phase that prevents 70% of downstream surprises by surfacing reality early — site condition, decision dynamics, real budget, real deadline.

Inquiry logged in Sales DB within 24 hrs with source tag.
Qualification call scheduled within 72 hrs · 7 questions, 15 min.
Discovery meeting with questionnaire pre-sent 48 hrs prior.
Site survey with 60–120 photos · hidden-condition flags.
Society pre-check · NOC tracker row created same day.
Project brief signed off via email · Decisions Log entry.
DSA executed · BB/RES/YYYY/NNN issued · ₹500 e-stamp.
20% retainer received before any design work begins.
Approval Gate A Signed DSA + 20% retainer received + Brief signed-off + Society NOC Tracker row created. Without all four, do not proceed.
B

Concept Design

Wk 3 — 4

Mood, layout, materiality. Two layouts for 2 BHK, three for 3/4 BHK — never more, never fewer. Constrain choice; prevent paralysis.

Concept deck built in CD format · narrative, mood, materiality.
Layout options with rationale + trade-off summaries.
Materiality palette referencing real Indian brands.
In-studio presentation — never over WhatsApp.
Revisions capped at R2 (2 BHK) / R3 (3/4 BHK).
Email sign-off with explicit acknowledgement language.
Decisions Log entry with PDF hash, revision number.
Payment 2 (20%) invoice issued same day.
Approval Gate B Concept sign-off email received + Payment 2 received + Materiality direction frozen.
C

Design Development

Wk 5 — 8/12

Eight sub-stages, each with its own approval. Floor plans, ceiling plans, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, 3D, samples, joinery details — sequenced and signed off in order, never as one block.

Working floor plans at 1:50 with 25mm tolerance.
RCP with beam overlay — clashes resolved before issue.
Switchboard schedule sheet — prevents 80% of point snags.
3D renders for high-stakes spaces only · 6/9-10/12-14.
Render disclaimer slide in every render PDF.
Physical samples · Approved Samples Log row each.
Mockup boards 2'×2' for PU shade and paint sheen.
5-day rule on every sample approval, written escalation thereafter.
Approval Gate C All Annexure A DD-stage rows ticked + Approved Samples Log >95% complete + Payment 3 (25%) received.
D

Documentation & BOQ

Wk 8 — 10/12

GFC drawings issued, BOQ prepared and approved, drawing register populated. The phase where Procurement Schedule is born and the top-3 long-lead items are flagged for the entire project.

GFC set issued · ~50 / 75 / 100 sheets by BHK.
Drawing Register populated with IFC status per sheet.
BOQ format chosen — Format A room-wise / Format B trade-wise.
18-category tree visited including 5–8% contingency.
Internal QC by PM and PA before client share.
BOQ presentation in person — explain 3 drivers, 3 levers.
Revision rounds capped at 2 / 2 / 3 by BHK.
Optimisation Sheet ready · KEEP/TRIM/CUT colour codes.
Approval Gate D GFC set issued (ISS-01) + BOQ approved + Payment 4 (20%) received + Drawing Register fully populated.
E

Procurement & Execution

Wk 8 — 17/24

13-step canonical execution sequence — out-of-sequence work is the #2 site-delay cause. Daily stand-ups, weekly site reports, change orders signed before any work proceeds.

3 quotes minimum per major category · vendor onboarding form.
Procurement Schedule with order-by dates · 1-wk buffer.
Festival overlay · Ganpati / Diwali / Holi avoided.
Monsoon overlay · wet trades pre-monsoon, polish post.
Society NOC in hand before site mobilisation.
Trade walkthroughs · 6 trades, signed acknowledgement.
QC walks photo-documented at every stage close.
Change Order discipline — no verbal approvals, ever.
Approval Gate E (rolling) Each of 13 stages closes only when its QC walk passes, photos are filed, and the next stage's GRNs are in hand.
F

Snagging & Handover

Wk 17 — 18/24

Two-pass snag walk, deep clean, styling, photoshoot, walkthrough, handover binder. The 12-month defects-liability period begins. 30/60/90-day reviews scheduled at handover, not later.

Two-pass snag walk 2 weeks before handover.
Critical = 0 · Major ≤ 5 · Minor ≤ 15 before client walk.
Deep clean by professional crew · every surface, hardware.
Styling install per pre-approved shot list.
Photoshoot within 7 days · 10–15 hero frames.
Handover binder · warranties, AMC, manuals, vendor contacts.
Lessons-Learned page drafted by LD · 1 page max.
30/60/90-day reviews scheduled at handover.
Approval Gate F Handover acknowledgement signed + Payment 5 (15%) received + Project archived + Lessons reviewed at next Monday Weekly Task Review.

03 / 06Delay × Mitigation Matrix

Twenty-one delay causes, each pre-empted.

Mumbai residential interiors rarely fail for one big reason — they slip through twenty small ones. The SOP names every common cause, ties it to the stage that prevents it, and lays down the escalation trigger and remediation path. Every checkbox above carries one of these tags.

D-01
Long-lead material delays
MitigationProcurement Schedule with order-by dates, 1-wk buffer, top-3 long-lead flagged at Stage 4 sign-off. Backup vendor activated by Day 7 of slip.
D-02
BOQ approval lag from clients
MitigationBOQ presented in person with optimisation sheet ready. Revision rounds capped. Formal slippage letter issued at 7 working days unread.
D-03
Mid-execution scope changes
MitigationChange Order discipline — no verbal approvals. Signed CO with cost and time impact before any work resumes. Running total visible weekly.
D-04
Vendor reliability — quality, time
MitigationVendor onboarding form, factory visit, two reference calls. Vendor agreement signed. Day-3 written escalation, Day-7 backup activation.
D-05
Society restrictions — hours, lifts
MitigationSociety NOC Tracker row created Day 1 of site visit. Submitted end of Stage 4. 9AM–6PM, no Sundays factored into schedule.
D-06
Monsoon impact (June–Sept)
MitigationWet trades sequenced pre-monsoon. Paint and PU reserved for post-monsoon Sept–Oct window. 15% schedule contingency added.
D-07
Client decision lag on samples
Mitigation5 working-day rule per sample. Samples dispatched in batches not one-by-one. Written escalation referencing day-for-day timeline impact.
D-08
Client payment milestone delays
MitigationDSA terms enforced — 1.5%/month late fee. Vendor payments paused with disclosure to client at 14 days overdue.
D-09
Civil surprises — load-bearing, seepage
MitigationHidden-condition flags at site survey. Pre-demo photos. Structural certificate before any wall is touched. Stop-work protocol on discovery.
D-10
Trade clashes — MEP / ceiling / carpentry
MitigationBeam-coordination overlay at C2. Switchboard schedule at C3. Trade-by-trade walkthroughs at site mobilisation, signed acknowledgement.
D-11
Festival labour shortages
MitigationFestival overlay on Procurement Schedule. Never schedule criticals during Ganpati / Diwali / Holi ± 2 wks. Pre-festival schedule freeze.
D-12
Society NOC delays
MitigationNOC Tracker started Day 1. Submitted end of Stage 4. Escalation to owner-member intervention if pending > 30 days non-structural.
D-13
Quality issues requiring rework
MitigationQC anchors per stage. Mockup discipline for high-risk finishes — 2'×2' boards approved before bulk. Vendor-accountability clause.
D-14
Sample approval delays
MitigationSame as D-07 — 5-day rule, batch dispatch, written escalation referencing day-for-day timeline impact.
D-15
Custom fabrication errors
MitigationHardware schedules with explicit Hettich/Häfele SKUs. GRN protocol — inspection before signing challan. Vendor rectifies at own cost.
D-16
Festival vendor shutdowns
MitigationOrders placed pre-festival. Payments cleared pre-festival. Backup vendor on standby. Balance held until reopen.
D-17
Client mind-changes after install
Mitigation3D renders + walkthrough video for high-stakes spaces. Render disclaimer slide. Mockup discipline. Post-install changes are CO with full impact.
D-18
LD juggling multiple projects
MitigationStudio capacity rule — 1 LD ≤ 3 active residential projects, ≤ 1 in execution. Monday Weekly Review monitors load. PA reassignment.
D-19
Inadequate site supervision
MitigationCadence baked in — 2× / 3× / daily by BHK. Weekly Site Visit Report mandatory. LD escalates own bandwidth proactively to PM.
D-20
Communication gaps
MitigationSingle LD = single SPOC. Written MOM after every meeting. Weekly SVR. Trade walkthroughs. Verbal-only changes trigger comms reset.
D-21
Last-minute additions on site
MitigationCO discipline. LD never says yes on site. 3rd unsigned verbal request triggers formal CO before any work proceeds.

04 / 06The Client Version

A calm, jargon-free walkthrough for the client.

Premium reassurance, set expectations honestly, share responsibility for what slows projects down. Eight minutes to read. Used at kickoff and referenced throughout.

A home is a deeply personal undertaking. This document explains exactly how we work together, what we'll need from you at each step, and how long things truly take in Mumbai. There are no surprises later because the surprises are named here, in advance.

Your project runs across six phases, in a roughly eighteen-week window for a 2 BHK, twenty-two weeks for a 3 BHK, and twenty-four to twenty-eight weeks for a 4 BHK — assuming we receive your approvals on time and the building society plays ball.

One Lead Designer owns your project end-to-end. Same person, first meeting to handover. You won't be passed between people. Vinit, our founder, signs off all drawings before they go to construction.

Approvals are the engine. Slow approvals stretch timelines more than any other single factor. We ask for a five working-day turnaround on every approval, and we track every decision in a shared Decisions Log so nothing is forgotten. Five payment milestones — twenty, twenty, twenty-five, twenty, fifteen — invoiced the same day we hit a milestone, payable within seven working days.
